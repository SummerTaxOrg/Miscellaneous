# Research Dossier: Senator Julian Cyr (D — Cape & Islands)

**Purpose.** Foundation for a letter urging Senator Cyr to author the "next fix" in his own
housing campaign: an exemption from the 6.25% sales and use tax on building materials for an
**owner-occupied primary residence**, modeled on the multifamily materials exemption he just
carried into law. This dossier maps who he is, what he has built, how he talks, who funds him,
what his district needs, and the procedural path a follow-on bill would travel — so the letter
can speak in his own language and to his own incentives.

**Compiled:** June 20, 2026. **Sources:** Massachusetts OCPF public API and filings;
malegislature.gov; senatorcyr.com; contemporaneous news (Nantucket Current, Boston Globe,
State House News, Vineyard Gazette, CommonWealth Beacon, and others). Inline URLs throughout.

---

## 1. Snapshot

Julian Cyr is the **Senate Chair of the Joint Committee on Housing** and a **Senate Assistant
Majority Whip**, representing the Cape and Islands district (Barnstable, Dukes, and Nantucket
counties). He is the author and public face of the **new sales-tax exemption on building
materials for multifamily affordable housing** (S.3041, signed June 12, 2026). Housing is his
signature issue, framed relentlessly around the survival of **year-round communities** on the
Cape and Islands. He is young (40), widely expected eventually to seek the MA-9 congressional
seat, and funded by a coalition that notably includes **building-trades unions, the realtors'
and commercial-development PACs, and the Massachusetts Retail Lumber Dealers Association.** Each
of these facts is a hook for the letter; the sections below document them.

## 5. ★ The enacted bill — the hook for the entire letter [CONFIRMED]

- **What it is:** a new sales/use tax **EXEMPTION** (not a credit) on building materials,
  supplies, and associated costs for approved **multifamily** housing projects. The letter must
  use "exemption," not "credit."
- **Vehicle:** **S.3041**, the FY26 Consolidated "Fair Share" Supplemental Budget.
  (https://malegislature.gov/Bills/194/S3041)
- **Cost:** capped at **$35 million per year**, per the Senate's own fact sheet.
  (https://malegislature.gov/PressRoom/Detail?pressReleaseId=350)
- **Eligibility:** projects with **≥15% affordable units**, *or* located where median household
  income is **below 120% of the state average.**
  (https://malegislature.gov/PressRoom/Detail?pressReleaseId=351)
- **Projected impact:** Senate estimates up to **35,000 units over 10 years**; materials are
  roughly **one-third** of an average ~$550,000 multifamily unit cost.
  (https://www.bostonglobe.com/2026/04/02/business/construction-sales-tax/)
- **Stated rationale:** rising construction costs from tariffs and supply-chain disruption.
  (https://nantucketcurrent.com/news/new-sales-tax-exemption-on-building-materials-for-affordable-housing-could-be-a-boon-for-nantucket)
- **Timeline:** Cyr filed a study amendment May 2025; Senate passed S.3041 **35–4 on April 9,
  2026**; **Gov. Healey signed June 12, 2026.**
  (https://malegislature.gov/PressRoom/Detail?pressReleaseId=351 ;
  https://www.nbcboston.com/news/politics/healey-signs-1-56-billion-spending-bill-into-law/3964651/)
- **Cyr's role:** the *Nantucket Current* states he "**authored and advanced** the measure
  alongside Senate President Karen Spilka," and State House News identifies him, as housing
  co-chair, as the proposer. **Nuance:** the formal sponsor of record on the budget vehicle is
  the **Senate Committee on Ways and Means**, and official Senate releases credit Spilka and
  Ways & Means rather than naming Cyr. Both framings are true at different levels; the policy
  is his, the vehicle was leadership's. The idea traces to the Senate's **"Unlocking Housing
  Production Commission."** [CONFIRMED policy authorship via news + role]
  (https://www.statehousenews.com/news/housing/spilka-eyes-sales-tax-exemption-to-encourage-housing/article_09a97e7e-0b47-4e49-8fca-e37844883a4f.html)
- **GAP:** the enacted **session-law chapter number** ("Chapter __ of the Acts of 2026") was
  not confirmed; verify on the malegislature.gov bill history before citing it in the letter.

## 6. Housing legislative record [CONFIRMED unless noted]

- **Seasonal Communities designation** — Cyr's signature contribution to the **2024 Affordable
  Homes Act**, creating specialized housing tools (ADUs by right, year-round housing trusts,
  deed restrictions, undersized-lot year-round units) for towns where ≥35% of units are second
  homes; all Martha's Vineyard and Nantucket towns auto-qualify.
  (https://www.senatorcyr.com/housing ;
  https://www.mvtimes.com/2024/08/07/islands-receive-special-designation-new-housing-law/)
- **S.967 → redrafted S.2981**, *An Act relative to housing in seasonal communities* — his
  continuing vehicle; reported favorably, referred to Senate Ways & Means (March 2026).
  (https://malegislature.gov/Bills/194/S2981)
- **Luxury real-estate transfer fee** — a local-option fee on high-value home sales to fund
  year-round housing, which he repeatedly champions; stripped from the 2024 bill but "not
  forever" off the table. (https://www.senatorcyr.com/housing ;
  https://provincetownindependent.org/featured/2024/07/02/senate-drops-transfer-fee-from-housing-bill/)
- **Other district wins he claims:** expanded Housing Court to Barnstable County; **tripled the
  Title V septic tax credit ($6,000 → $18,000)** and doubled lead-abatement credits; a 2023
  municipal property-tax exemption for landlords renting year-round at affordable rates.
  (https://www.senatorcyr.com/housing)
- **Sponsored-bill list (194th)** includes S.965 (fair housing), S.966 (seasonal-community
  funding / investor mitigation), S.1433 (multifamily zoning reform), S.1434 (transfer fee),
  S.1955 (Martha's Vineyard housing bank). [Profile listing; verify individual pairings]
  (https://malegislature.gov/Legislators/Profile/JAC0/Bills/Sponsored)

**Reading:** He already believes the core premise of our ask — that **lowering the cost to
build** is sound housing policy and that **tax tools** are a legitimate lever. He has used tax
credits (Title V), tax exemptions (the materials bill), and tax fees (transfer fee) as housing
instruments. The owner-occupant materials exemption is squarely within his demonstrated method.

## 9. District housing dynamics [CONFIRMED; price figures approximate]

The Cape and Islands have **among the most extreme housing economics in the United States**, which
makes the cost-of-building argument land harder here than almost anywhere:

- **Nantucket:** medians in the **multi-million-dollar** range (sources vary: reported medians
  from ~$2.3M to ~$3.3M; averages higher). Among the most expensive markets in the country.
  (https://www.zillow.com/home-values/397237/nantucket-ma/)
- **Martha's Vineyard:** 2025 median **~$1.7M**, up >30% since 2021; little available under $1M.
  (https://vineyardgazette.com/news/2026/01/22/island-real-estate-market-sees-steady-expensive-growth)
- **Cape Cod (Barnstable County):** median home prices **exceed $760,000.**
  (https://guthrieschofieldgroup.com/blog/the-cape-cod-housing-crunch-how-wealthy-vacation-home-buyers-are-reshaping-the-market)
- **Second-home / STR drag:** ~1 in 10 Cape homes is a short-term rental and ~25% are seasonal/
  occasional-use — stock pulled from the year-round market. Two months of STR revenue can exceed
  a year of long-term rent. (same source; Cape Cod Commission Regional Housing Needs Assessment,
  https://www.capecodcommission.org/our-work/regional-housing-needs-assessment/)
- **★ Island construction-cost premium (directly amplifies the materials-tax argument):**
  building costs run **$550–$850+/sq ft on the Cape and $650–$1,200+/sq ft on the islands**, with
  a **10–20% premium over comparable mainland projects** driven by ferrying materials, logistics,
  and housing the trades — plus **$372/ton** just to dispose of construction debris on Nantucket.
  Because materials cost *more* here, the 6.25% tax on them costs Cyr's constituents *more* than
  it costs the average Massachusetts family.
  (https://www.mcelhinneycustomhomes.com/post/custom-home-building-costs-on-martha-s-vineyard-cape-cod-2025-guide ;
  https://fishernantucket.com/construction-on-nantucket-part-4-cost-per-square-foot/)
- **Workforce squeeze:** Cape Cod Healthcare reported ~508 open positions, with housing cited as
  a recruiting barrier; the crisis is shrinking the regional labor force.
  (https://www.capenews.net/falmouth/news/staff-levels-improving-at-cape-cod-healthcare-but-housing-is-a-barrier/article_638c8328-3a29-59dc-a8c6-cbf91743cf3a.html)

## 10. Allies and the legislative path [CONFIRMED]

- **Key allies:** Senate President **Karen Spilka** (housing a stated 2026 priority; co-author of
  the materials exemption); **Lydia Edwards** (Housing Committee Senate Vice Chair, frequent
  co-filer); his Cape & Islands House counterpart is now **Rep. Thomas Moakley** (sworn in Jan.
  2025; note **Dylan Fernandes moved to the Senate** — do not describe Fernandes as the House
  member). (https://malegislature.gov/Committees/Detail/J28 ;
  https://www.mvtimes.com/2025/01/03/moakley-sworn-island-state-representative/)
- **Path for a tax-exemption bill:** tax/revenue matters route through the **Joint Committee on
  Revenue**, then **Ways and Means**, then the floor (three readings), then the second chamber.
  A **constitutional wrinkle:** revenue "money bills" must originate in the **House**; a tax
  exemption is generally handled on the revenue/Ways-and-Means track, which is why the multifamily
  exemption rode a **budget vehicle** rather than a standalone bill.
  (https://www.masslegalservices.org/content/legislative-process-massachusetts-0)
- **Tactical implication:** the cleanest route for an owner-occupant exemption may again be a
  **budget amendment or supplemental-budget rider**, exactly as the multifamily version traveled,
  rather than a standalone bill that must clear committee on its own. The letter can note that the
  precedent is not only substantive but **procedural** — the vehicle already exists.

## 11. Strategic implications for the letter (the lobbying read)

1. **Lead with his own words and his own bill.** Open on "There's no one fix" and the
   multifamily exemption he just won. Position the owner-occupant exemption as the explicit
   **next fix** — the same tool, the same logic, the missing half.
2. **Echo his vocabulary:** *year-round families, working families and employers, faster and
   cheaper to build, profoundly unaffordable, the homes Massachusetts needs.* Mirror, don't
   parrot.
3. **Make it local.** The island construction-cost premium (10–20%, plus $372/ton debris,
   plus ferried materials) means the materials tax bites his constituents *harder*. The
   year-round Nantucket/Vineyard family building or rebuilding a modest home pays the 6.25% on
   the most expensive materials in the Commonwealth.
4. **Reassure on revenue and fairness.** He accepted $35M/yr for multifamily; an owner-occupant
   exemption confined to primary residences (no second homes, no developers, no resale) is
   bounded and aligns perfectly with his stated fight *against* the second-home market that
   drives his region's prices. This is a feature he will recognize as his own.
5. **His coalition is already aligned.** Building trades, realtors, commercial developers, and
   the **lumber dealers** all fund him and all benefit. The ask adds homeowners and GCs to that
   tent without subtracting anyone.
6. **Offer the procedural precedent.** The multifamily exemption rode a supplemental-budget
   amendment; the owner-occupant version can travel the same way. He knows the route.
7. **Tone.** Respectful, peer-to-policymaker, crediting him for the multifamily win and inviting
   him to complete the arc. He responds to "common sense" framing and to being the doer who
   "has the guts to do it."
8. **Avoid:** calling the multifamily measure a "credit" (it is an exemption); claiming he was
   the formal *sponsor of record* (he is the policy *author*; Ways & Means is the vehicle's
   sponsor); asserting he is running for Congress; calling him "first" openly LGBTQ senator;
   citing a chapter number not yet verified.
