# 01 — Formatting and Rules: How a Massachusetts Amicus Brief Is Built

This document records the procedural research that governs the form of the brief at
[`/AMICUS-BRIEF.md`](../../AMICUS-BRIEF.md). Massachusetts amicus practice is set by the
**Massachusetts Rules of Appellate Procedure** — principally **Rule 17** (amicus briefs),
with **Rule 16** (brief contents) and **Rule 20** (form and length) incorporated.

---

## I. Rule 17 — Brief of an Amicus Curiae

### Who may file, and how

A brief of an amicus curiae may be filed only:
1. **by leave of the appellate court or a single justice**, granted on motion;
2. **when solicited by the appellate court** (the SJC's "amicus announcements"); or
3. **if the Commonwealth or one of its officers or agencies is an amicus.**

A **motion for leave** must identify the applicant's interest and state why an amicus
brief is desirable. (The brief is drafted to be filed with such a motion, or with the
assent of the parties — see the cover footnote.)

### Timing

An amicus must file **no later than 21 days before the date of oral argument**, unless the
court grants leave for later filing for cause shown.

### What the cover must say

The cover must **identify the party or parties supported** and **state whether the brief
supports affirmance, reversal, or neither**. (Our cover: "IN SUPPORT OF THE
PLAINTIFF-APPELLANT AND REVERSAL.")

### What an amicus brief must contain — and what it is excused from

An amicus brief must comply with Rule 20 and must include the enumerated Rule 16 items,
**but it is excused** from the statements of the case, of the facts, and of the standard
of review that a party's brief requires. In practice the required front matter is:

- **Corporate disclosure statement** — Rule 17(c)(1) (if the amicus is a corporation), a
  statement like that required of parties by **S.J.C. Rule 1:21**.
- **Rule 17(c)(5) declaration** — the authorship-and-funding declaration (see § IV below).
- **Table of contents** — Rule 16(a)(3).
- **Table of authorities** — Rule 16(a)(4).
- **Statement of the interest of the amicus.**
- **Summary of argument** (required where the argument exceeds 20 pages or 4,500 words).
- **Argument.**
- **Certificate of compliance** (Rule 16(k)) and **certificate of service.**

## II. Rule 20 — Form and length (the numbers the certificate must hit)

| Requirement | Amicus brief |
|---|---|
| **Cover color** | **Green.** (Appellant = blue; appellee = red; reply = gray.) Color requirement does not apply to e-filed briefs, but the convention is observed. |
| **Length** | Either **≤ 35 pages** (monospaced font) **or ≤ 7,500 words** (proportionally spaced font). This is roughly half a party's principal-brief allowance. |
| **Summary of argument** | Required if the argument exceeds **20 pages / 4,500 words**. |
| **Certificate of compliance** | Rule 16(k): must **state the method** used to determine length — the font, the non-excluded **word count**, and the counting method. |

## III. The section order (modeled on a real SJC amicus brief)

The brief follows the exact ordering used in a recent SJC amicus filing
(*Gotay* v. *Malpass*, SJC-13666, Brief of Amici Curiae CPCS & CLCM, Nov. 15, 2024):

```
COVER  (court · docket · caption · title identifying party supported · counsel block · date)
TABLE OF CONTENTS
TABLE OF AUTHORITIES        (Constitutional Provisions · Cases · Statutes/Regs · Other Authorities)
CORPORATE DISCLOSURE STATEMENT      (Rule 17(c)(1))
RULE 17(c)(5) DECLARATION
STATEMENT OF THE INTEREST OF THE AMICI CURIAE
[ISSUE ADDRESSED]
SUMMARY OF ARGUMENT          (with parenthetical pin-cites to argument pages)
ARGUMENT                     (point headings I, II, … ; subheadings A, B, …)
CONCLUSION                   (the prayer for relief)
CERTIFICATE OF COMPLIANCE
CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE
ADDENDUM                     (constitutional / statutory texts)
```

Two conventions worth copying from the model brief: the **counsel signature block sits on
the cover** (with BBO numbers), and the **summary of argument carries parenthetical page
references** to where each point is developed (e.g., "*(Argument I.)*").

## IV. The two declarations — copied to the letter

**Corporate disclosure (Rule 17(c)(1))**, adapted from the model brief's nonprofit form:

> Pursuant to Mass. R. App. P. 17(c)(1), amicus curiae [name] states that it is a nonprofit
> membership corporation … has no parent corporation, … issues no stock, and … no publicly
> held company owns ten percent or more of its stock.

**Rule 17(c)(5) declaration**, tracking the model brief nearly verbatim:

> No party or party's counsel authored this brief in whole or in part; no party, party's
> counsel, or other person or entity other than the amici and their counsel contributed
> money intended to fund preparing or submitting this brief; and none of the amici or
> their counsel represents or has represented one of the parties to the present appeal in
> another proceeding involving similar issues, or was a party or represented a party in a
> proceeding or legal transaction that is at issue in the present appeal.

This 17(c)(5) declaration is the rule's anti-"party-funded brief" safeguard; the amici must
be the genuine authors and funders of their own brief.

## V. Service note specific to this case

Because the underlying action is a constitutional attack on a statute, **G.L. c. 231A, § 8
requires that the Attorney General be served** and entitled to be heard. The certificate of
service reflects service on the Attorney General's office (which also represents the
Commissioner of Revenue) in addition to the parties.
