# 01 — Format and Design of a Legislative One-Pager

This document records the conventions the leave-behind at [`/ONE-PAGER.md`](../../ONE-PAGER.md)
is built to, drawn from advocacy and government-relations guidance on one-pagers and
"leave-behinds."

## I. The non-negotiables

| Rule | Why | In our piece |
|---|---|---|
| **One physical page.** | It is a leave-behind, read in seconds by busy staff. Two pages is a memo, not a one-pager. | Content sized to one printed page; a trim note is included if a letterhead is added. |
| **The ask, up front.** | The reader must learn what you want before anything else. Reference a specific bill. | A boxed **"THE ASK"** at the top, naming a (placeholder) bill number and title. |
| **Scannable, not dense.** | Skimmed, not read. Bullets, short blocks, strong headers. | Bold section headers; 2–3 bullets each; no paragraph longer than a few lines. |
| **Evidence, briefly.** | Back the ask with a fact or number, not an essay. | A single illustrative cost figure and the 2026 exemption comparison. |
| **No exhaustive analysis.** | A one-pager makes the case, it does not argue every counterpoint. | Counterarguments are left to the memo; the page makes one clean argument. |
| **Who you are + contact.** | Staff must be able to follow up. | Endorser line, sponsor line, and a contact block at the foot. |

## II. The section order used

```
TITLE BANNER          — a short, arresting name ("TAXED AT THE THRESHOLD")
TAGLINE               — one line stating the whole argument
THE ASK  (boxed)      — the specific legislative request + bill cite
THE PROBLEM           — 3 bullets: what the tax is and whom it hits
THE DOUBLE STANDARD   — the strongest point, with a pull-quote
WHAT IT COSTS A FAMILY— one illustrative number (flagged)
THE FIX               — what the bill does; narrow scope; fiscal framing
WHY NOW               — the timeliness / political hook
CONSTITUTIONAL QUOTE  — the Art. 23 / Adams pull-quote (authority + resonance)
FOOTER                — endorsers, sponsor, contact, date, caveat line
```

## III. Presentation and typography (for the rendered/printed version)

The markdown is written so it converts cleanly to a formatted one-pager. When producing the
final document (Word, Google Docs, or a designed PDF):

- **Hierarchy:** a large bold title, a one-line italic tagline, then bold ALL-CAPS section
  headers so the eye can jump between blocks.
- **The ask as a box.** Render the "THE ASK" blockquote as a shaded/bordered call-out so it
  is the first thing seen. Do the same for the confrontational pull-quote.
- **Pull-quotes** (the "developer's materials… / a family still pays" line and the Art. 23
  quotation) set larger or indented, to break the page and carry the emotional beats.
- **Restraint with color and none with emoji.** A single accent color for headers and the
  ask-box border is enough; avoid emoji and clip-art, which read as unserious to a
  legislative audience. The lone `▶` marker on "THE ASK" is the only ornament.
- **White space is a feature**, not wasted room — it is what makes the page feel like a
  ninety-second read rather than a wall.
- **Letterhead.** Add the coalition's logo/letterhead at the top; if it crowds the page,
  drop the "What It Costs" or "Why Now" block first.

## IV. Distribution note

A one-pager is meant to be (a) handed over in a meeting after the verbal pitch, and
(b) emailed as a PDF attachment or pasted into the body. Keep a tagged-PDF version for
accessibility and a plain-text version for email bodies.
