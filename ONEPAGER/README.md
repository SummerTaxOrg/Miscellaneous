# ONE-PAGER — Legislative Leave-Behind

This directory holds the formatting research and content notes behind the one-page
legislator explainer at [`/ONE-PAGER.md`](../ONE-PAGER.md).

## What this is

The fifth work product on a single cause. The
[memo](../MA-owner-builder-sales-tax-constitutional-memo.md) is the legal analysis; the
[speech](../SPEECH.md) is the public oration; the [amicus brief](../AMICUS-BRIEF.md) is the
court filing; the [op-ed](../OP-ED.md) is the citizen's newspaper voice. **This is the
document you hand a legislator or staffer** — a single page, built to be read in ninety
seconds and to leave the ask behind in writing.

## The client's request

> Generate a fierce one-page explainer for legislators, being mindful of formatting and
> presentation styles.

## How "fierce" is balanced against credibility

A one-pager that overstates loses the room; one that is timid is forgotten. The piece is
fierce in **structure and framing**, not in adjectives:

- It leads with the **double standard** — the Legislature already exempted developers' materials
  in 2026 but left families paying — and puts that contradiction in the legislator's lap.
- It uses **short, declarative headers** and a confrontational pull-quote ("A developer's
  materials now come tax-free. A family building its own home still pays.").
- It stays **credible**: every claim ties to the underlying research, the dollar example is
  openly flagged as illustrative, and the ask is narrow and already-modeled, not sweeping.

See [`research/02-content-and-tone.md`](research/02-content-and-tone.md).

## Caveats

- **Placeholders to fill and verify.** Bill number, sponsor, coalition name, and contact line
  are bracketed. The dollar figure is anchored on a **single reputable source** (NAHB 2024
  *Cost of Constructing a Home*, ~$428k build cost) with a conservative ~40% materials share
  as the one derived input; see [`research/03`](research/03-cost-figures.md). Confirm against
  the latest survey before citing, and label the materials share as derived wherever quoted.
- **Length has outgrown one page.** Successive review rounds added substance (the
  compounding-markup explanation, the visibility reframe, the administrability rebuttal, the
  exemption-mechanism detail, the cost figure, and the closing "Choice" section plus two
  founder quotations). The text now runs ~1,000 words, roughly 1.25–1.5 pages at normal
  formatting. Two honest choices before distribution: (a) **hold one page** by trimming —
  drop one founder quotation, fold the cost block into a single sentence, and tighten the
  problem bullets; or (b) **let it become a tight two-page brief**, which the added content
  arguably now warrants. Flagging rather than silently cutting requested material.
- **Advocacy, not advice.** Like its companions, it argues the cause at its most persuasive;
  the memo remains the candid assessment of the legal odds.
