# TAXED AT THE THRESHOLD

### Massachusetts charges families the sales tax on the very materials of the home they are building. It already stopped charging apartment developers. Finish the job.

---

> ### ▶ THE ASK
> **Pass [Bill No. S./H. ____], _An Act exempting owner-occupied home-construction materials from the sales and use tax._** Exempt from the 6.25% sales/use tax the building materials a Massachusetts resident buys to build or substantially rehabilitate their **own primary residence** — the same relief the Legislature granted multifamily developers in 2026.

---

**THE PROBLEM**

- Massachusetts taxes the raw materials of a house. Every stud, window, wire, and length of pipe bought to build a primary home carries the **6.25%** sales tax — or an equal use tax on anything bought out of state.
- It lands directly on the families doing the work themselves. An owner-builder pays it **out of pocket**, on top of land, labor, and financing. When a contractor is hired, the state's own "contractor-as-consumer" rule makes the builder pay it and fold it straight into the family's price.
- This is a tax on shelter, not on luxury. You cannot raise a wall without the things being taxed.

**THE DOUBLE STANDARD — YOU ALREADY FIXED HALF OF IT**

In 2026 the Legislature exempted roughly **$35 million a year** in building materials for qualifying **multifamily** projects. Same lumber. Same nails. Same delivery trucks.

> A developer's materials now come tax-free. A family building its own home still pays.
> **The Commonwealth has already conceded both the principle and the budget — it simply left families out.**

**WHAT IT COSTS A FAMILY** *(illustrative — replace with verified figures)*

- ~**$[150,000]** in taxable materials for a typical single-family home → about **$[9,375]** in sales/use tax per house, paid before anyone moves in.

**THE FIX — NARROW, TARGETED, ALREADY-MODELED**

- Adds an **owner-occupant exemption** to G.L. c. 64H, § 6, drafted to parallel the exemptions already on the books.
- **Tightly limited:** natural persons only; a **primary residence** only; **not** developers, speculators, or resale construction.
- **Modest fiscal note** beside the multifamily carve-out the Legislature already accepted, and a direct cut to the cost of building and rehabbing homes.

**WHY NOW**

Housing cost is the top concern of Massachusetts voters. This bill lowers the price of building and rehabilitating homes, rewards the families and tradespeople who actually build them, and ends a split that taxes the homeowner while sparing the developer.

> *"No subsidy, charge, tax, impost, or duties, ought to be... levied... without the consent of the people, or their representatives in the legislature."*
> — Massachusetts Declaration of Rights, Article 23 (drafted by John Adams, 1780)
